Project Requirements

This week, I worked on defining the requirements for the design ideation poster that is part of Assessment 3. I focused on ensuring that my design concept fits the assignment's objectives, including dataset usage, target audience, and interactive features.

Ideation

This week, I expanded on my initial ideation process by exploring more innovative ways to present the data from public sources. I used sketches to visualize different user interactions, including options like an interactive map, a carousel of data-driven images, and a gamified experience. I found that ideating multiple approaches helped in narrowing down a compelling concept that would be both visually engaging and informative.

Research

I conducted in-depth research on data-driven visualization projects, focusing on best practices for interactive design. I studied examples from popular platforms and projects to gain insights into how they used data to tell a compelling story. This helped me refine my approach and solidify my design direction. I also continued to review cultural sensitivity guidelines to ensure that my work is inclusive and mindful of all audiences, especially when working with sensitive data.

My Contribution

This week, I contributed by refining the sketches from last week, incorporating feedback I received from peers and instructors. I also worked on building a more detailed draft of the design ideation poster, adding in specific elements such as the finalized title and visual representations of my concept. My primary contribution was ensuring that my ideation aligns with the project requirements, which was a significant step towards the final poster.

Reflection

Reflecting on this week's progress, I learned the importance of iteration in the design process. Initial sketches can evolve significantly through feedback, and each version becomes more refined. The feedback I received highlighted gaps I had not noticed, particularly in how the audience might interact with the data. Moving forward, I will continue to embrace iteration, ensuring I am open to constructive criticism to make the project more user-focused and engaging.
