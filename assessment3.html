Project Requirements

This week, I focused on creating a design ideation poster for my individual project as part of Assessment 3. The requirement is to produce a one-page A3 poster to present a design concept, which will be used for my presentation. The poster must include the title of my concept, low fidelity sketches, research of similar artifacts, and a brief description of the design concept, audience, and its value.

Ideation

For this design ideation poster, I started by brainstorming various concepts that could address an interesting problem using datasets from public sources like Brisbane City Council. The primary goal was to create an engaging way to represent this data to a specific target audience. I sketched three different low-fidelity concepts focusing on different approaches to interacting with the dataset: one for visual storytelling, one for interactive exploration, and one for gamification.

Research

I conducted research on similar existing applications and websites that visualize public datasets. This research helped me understand current design standards, interaction models, and methods of presenting information. I focused on ensuring that my design would not only be visually appealing but also respectful to the dataset and its intended audience. Additionally, I reviewed First Nations data guidelines to ensure cultural sensitivity in my representation if I included any related data.

My Contribution

My contribution to this week's work involved creating detailed sketches of the design concepts and drafting a brief description of each. I also prepared a standpoint statement to reflect my position in relation to the project. This exercise helped me clarify my role in the design process and understand how my perspective influences the representation of the data.

Reflection

Reflecting on this week's activities, I learned the importance of respectful data representation, especially when dealing with sensitive datasets such as First Nations data. The process of ideation and research helped me think critically about the potential impact of my design choices on different stakeholders. Moving forward, I plan to iterate on the design sketches based on peer feedback to ensure that the final poster is engaging, culturally sensitive, and effectively communicates the value of my concept.
