Project Requirements

This week, I started working on my Annotated Process Portfolio for DECO1800/7180. The requirements include creating a personal portfolio from scratch without using pre-made templates or frameworks like Bootstrap or React. The focus is on demonstrating my skills in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, ensuring all content is original and showcasing my individual contributions.

Ideation

I explored various inspirations for my portfolio’s design. I referred to examples from previous students, including Anastasia Laczko, Connor Wiegmink, and Michelle Owen, to understand different styles and approaches. I also looked at websites such as CSS Zen Garden, Behance, and Abduzeedo to brainstorm potential design elements that I could incorporate into my portfolio. The design inspiration helped me shortlist elements like minimalistic navigation, a cohesive color scheme, and interaction through hover effects.

Research

I reviewed resources on Adobe Color to develop a cohesive color palette for the website. I also explored CSS techniques to enhance the portfolio's usability and visual aesthetics, such as creating sticky navigation bars and using JavaScript animations. Researching different elements was insightful, and I felt excited as I experimented with color schemes and layouts.

My Contribution

My contributions to the team project so far include creating sketches of the different possible layouts for my portfolio. I made three different sketches that feature variations in navigation placement and how content is presented. I chose one that felt the most natural to the intended flow of my portfolio, focusing on user experience.

Reflection

This week, I worked on the planning phase of my portfolio by gathering inspiration, creating sketches, and deciding on key design elements. Initially, I felt overwhelmed by the variety of design directions I could take, but as I finalized my layout, I felt much more focused. Reviewing examples of past portfolios was beneficial, as it helped me avoid making beginner mistakes. In the future, I need to delve deeper into understanding the technical implementation of designs rather than just analyzing their aesthetics.
